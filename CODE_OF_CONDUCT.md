# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Nova Systems Lab community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility and apologizing for mistakes;
- focusing on what is best for the community;
- communicating clearly and professionally.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption;
- discriminatory conduct;
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

Examples include:

- GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions;
- project chat spaces and forums;
- project events and meetings;
- official email;
- social media when representing Nova Systems Lab.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported privately to:

**info@novasystemslab.org**

Security vulnerabilities should instead be reported to:

**security@novasystemslab.org**

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as practical. The privacy and safety of reporters will be respected.

## Enforcement guidelines

Project leaders may use the following responses depending on context, severity, and history:

### 1. Correction

A private or public warning explaining the inappropriate behavior and the expected correction.

### 2. Warning

A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. Temporary restrictions may be imposed.

### 3. Temporary ban

A temporary ban from interaction or participation in project spaces.

### 4. Permanent ban

Permanent removal from project spaces for sustained, severe, or repeated violations.
